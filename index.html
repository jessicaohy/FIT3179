Jessica's COVID19 Visualisations

Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID19) is an infectious disease that has spread all over the world. This pandemic has affected many innocent people and caused a large number of deaths. A few factors that affects the number of cases and deaths of CODI19 were explored and illustrated in these visualisations.

North America and South America have the highest number of cases per 100,000 people

The darker the shade of the area on the choropleth map, the larger the number of cases. United States of America, Brazil and Argentina have the darkest shade. This means that they have the highest number of cases per 100,000. Hovering over the diffent parts of the map shows information of each country.

United States of America has the highest number of deaths and cases

The number of deaths and new cases for each country are plotted into a bubble plot. In addition to these, the size of the circle corresponds to the population density of the country. Other countries with prominent number of cases were also labelled such as China, where COVID19 started from. Filters can also be applied to select certain continents or based on conditions.

Number of COVID19 cases in United States of America, Brazil and India generally increased from January 2020 to October 2020

As shown on the bubble plot above, a number of countries were prominent due to reasons such as having a high number of cases. These countries were plotted on a line chart to see the trend over a period of 10 months, from January 2020 to October 2020. In September 2020, India has the highest record of COVID19 cases compared to other countried within these 10 months. These countries can be filtered.

Highest number of death cases lies within median age group of 30s

Number of death cases in each country with their median age group are plotted on a bar graph. The highest number of death cases is within the median age group of 30s and the least number of death cases is within the median age group of 10s.

Generally, the more the number of hospital beds, the lesser the number of deaths

The scatterplot shows that many countries do not have many hospital beds. Counties with more hospital beds generally have lesser number of deaths. Continents can be filtered as well.
